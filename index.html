Key Findings

What does the AusPlay data reveal about participation in Victoria?

Participation is high overall, but significant inequalities remain across geography, income, education and disability status.

First, participation is widespread across Victoria, with almost nine in ten adults engaging in physical activity each year.


Second, informal activities such as walking, fitness training and recreational exercise now play a larger role than traditional organised sport.


Third, participation opportunities are not equally distributed. Lower-income households, people with disabilities, Aboriginal and Torres Strait Islander Australians, and residents of remote regions consistently report lower participation levels.


These findings suggest future policy should focus not only on increasing participation, but also on improving accessibility, affordability and inclusion for underrepresented groups.
